Our Design Philosophy

At Saarthi, our design philosophy is driven by a mission to create barrier-free digital experiences tailored for diverse human needs. We focus on age-inclusive and disability-responsive design, ensuring that children, teens, adults, and the elderly—including those with physical, sensory, intellectual, and developmental challenges—can navigate effortlessly. Every element, from content tone to interaction flow, is curated to uphold empathy, dignity, and respect for both special needs persons (SNP) and their caregivers.

We adopt an accessibility-first approach by implementing WCAG 2.1 AA-compliant high-contrast color palettes, a High Contrast Mode toggle for low-vision users, and clean sans-serif fonts like Open Sans and Lato with scalable sizing options. Navigation is optimized for keyboard, touch, and assistive technologies, while our Screen Reader Mode provides descriptive alt text, logical reading sequences, and instant activation without reload.

In usability, we emphasize predictable navigation and cognitive comfort—minimizing dropdowns, maintaining consistent menus, enlarging click targets, and introducing gentle, non-distracting animations. Content is categorized by age group and disability type, with multiple accessible formats: downloadable PDFs, audio, captioned videos, sign-language integrations, and clear, non-stereotypical icons for instant recognition.
